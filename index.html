About Me

Well, son, I’ll tell you: Life for me ain’t been no crystal stair, - Langston Hughes

Hi there! I’m Dmitri, a Computer Science and Spanish Literature double major at UNC Chapel Hill. While that may all sound great, my life has not always been a crystal stair.

I was born in this little country called Moldova. You may not have heard of it; it’s that little stip of land between Romania and the Ukraine. Thanks to my mom, she and I were able to move to the US when I was just 7. My level of English competency was on par with that of an average first grader’s Spanish skills in the US. Not very good, to say the least. They just threw me into second grade and told me, and I’m paraphrasing: We’re gonna give you a year to prove yourself in a regular classroom, or else we’ll demote you to first grade. Sounds like a challenge, huh? Well, I sure did prove it to them. Within a year, I was taking advanced placement English classes like a boss!

Let’s skip ahead to high school. For the past ten years or so of my life, I had been pretty darn sure that I wanted to go into the aerospace industry and become an engineer, like both of my parents. Up to that point, I had never really given progamming a chance. Well luckily, my uncle was an up-and-coming programmer and he thought it would be a great idea to send me a book on coding. I opened it and quite obviously, I didn’t understand a char[] of it (sorry, bad programmer joke). But then, as if by fate, I saw programming on my high school class catalog and I just knew that I had to take it. And guess what? I fell in love with it. The ability to mould the digital world around you is incredibly satisfying. For the first time in my life, I truly felt inspired to become someone great. Not great in terms of riches and fame, no, but rather, someone who is able to make a difference.
